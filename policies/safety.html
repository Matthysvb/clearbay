Safety Policy

How we look after your home and our crew on every job.

Working Safely in Your Home

Every GarageClean crew member is trained to work carefully in residential environments. We treat your home the way we'd want someone to treat ours — no rushing, no shortcuts, and we stop and think before we touch anything we're unsure about.

Hazardous Materials

Garages collect things that can be dangerous to move without proper handling — gasoline, old paint, propane canisters, pesticides, and similar items. We won't touch, transport, or dispose of these. If we find them on the job, we'll point them out and let you know how to get them handled properly. This isn't us being difficult — it's a legal and safety requirement we take seriously.

When We Stop Work

If we arrive and find conditions that make it unsafe to proceed — whether that's structural issues, pest activity, the presence of hazardous materials, or anything else that poses a real risk — we'll let you know immediately. You won't be charged for work that wasn't done. We'd rather lose a job than put your home or our crew in a bad situation.

What We Need From You

  • Let us know about any known safety issues before the job starts — even if you're not sure it matters
  • Keep pets somewhere secure and away from the garage while we're working
  • Make sure we have clear access to the space and to water and power if needed
  • Flag anything fragile, load-bearing, or that needs careful handling

Products We Use

We work with professional-grade cleaning products. Where we have a choice, we lean toward biodegradable and low-VOC options. If you or anyone in the household has allergies or chemical sensitivities, let us know when you book and we'll adjust what we bring.

If Something Happens

Our crew is trained to respond appropriately to any incident on the job. If you see something that concerns you while we're working, flag it immediately and we'll address it. Safety isn't a checkbox — it's how we operate.
